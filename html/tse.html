TSE @ UCSD

Website Redesign for TSE

May 2019 - June 2019

Role - UX/UI Designer/Researcher

Teammate - Sumeet Bansal

Overview

This last spring, I was contacted by the president of Triton Software Engineering (TSE) to redesign their organization’s website. I worked heavily with the other board members as well as the president to polish their website.

TSE an on campus organization that is dedicated to using technology for social good. Their primary audience are non-profits around the world who either don’t have the resources or the expertise to do the work themselves.

DESIGN PROCESS

Empathize

Why Redesign?

My first meeting with Sumeet was focused primarily on why he, and the rest of the organization, felt that their website needed a redesign. The following are the core points:


Aesthetic was outdated.

When their initial site launched they had multiple developer quickly putting it together so a lot of pages are inconsistent.

On top of that, the website content itself is outdated. Since 2017, TSE has grown tremendously and actually have worked on projects which should be showcased.

They knew who their audience was (students/non-profits), and felt that the website wasn't curated for them.

Overall design felt bleak and distant. TSE's mission is for social good and community building, and Sumeet wanted the site to express that.

ABOUT

What is TSE?

Non-profit software engineering organization focused on helping other non-profits by building them technological tools that they lack the resources to build themselves.

On campus organization consisting of students of all skill levels. Highly promotes community building and how each member learns and develops their skills during their time in TSE.

Aims to create customzied solutions to best aid other organizations.

Competitive Analysis

Sumeet had already linked me several other campus organization websites that were aiming to do the same thing as TSE (Blueprint, CS+Social Good). He emphasized that their layout was basically what he wanted, but was open to what I could come up with.

I did a lot of research on their navigation, the colors they chose to use, and the overall tone of their website. I also looked at other non-profit organizations and how their websites were formatted.

Goals for Redesign

I went ahead and interviewed multiple members on the board, including Sumeet, to holistically understand what they imagine the goals for the website redesign to be. These would then further guide my design process and also solidify the metrics that TSE wants to improve upon.

Usability

 Improve the browsing experience so that users can easily navigate through the page and find what information they are looking for efficiently.


Conversion

 By enhancing the overall design and implementing features that competing organizations have on their website TSE hopes to increase the amount of people who are interested in applying for both students and non-profits.


Brand

 Completely refresh the visual brand for their website which would hopefully attract more visitors. Having a more consistent style guide will also build on TSE’s personality and pave the way for other branding materials (flyers, event pages, business cards, etc.).

Architecture

Based on the research I had done, TSE’s architecture wasn’t bad, it was just missing a lot of pieces. The website felt very bare bones. The new IA I created would allow users to find content effortlessly, either by having appropriate placement of links/sections or adding in sections that were missing before.

Old IA

New IA

Oftentimes we obsess about simplifying things, and that by keeping it simple, it becomes easier for users to complete a task. However, TSE’s website was too simple. They had so few sections that it was impossible to accomplish things like building a brand or showcasing their work. All of these aspects are critical for TSE’s success as they can act as reasons for people to prefer TSE over another organization.

Wireframes

Once I felt confident on where to go with the website I started creating basic wireframes to send to Sumeet. I decided that designing both desktop and mobile would be a good idea because we anticipated that both will be very common mediums for visitors. Many people do their actual work on a larger screen, but at the same time, if visitors simply wanted to c heck what TSE is to get some information, this could be through their phone.

Mobile/Web Wireframes

User Testing

Once I got most of the content, I ran between-subject study with four people in each group. The first group tested on the original website while the second group tested on the redesign. I wanted to do the same for the mobile screens, but unfortunately the original TSE website was not responsive.

Redesign Group

Original Group

Key Findings

Users from the redesign were able to give a far more comprehensive explanation as to what TSE is and the type of work that they do.

Users from both designs had a lot of questions about TSE.

Users from the original design commented on how sloppy the ‘Connect’ page was, while users from the redesign felt that the pages were all very ‘modern’ and ‘start-up-y’

AB Test

The main issue that remained was that users still had questions about TSE, and there wasn’t a reliable way for them to answer these questions (besides contacting TSE).

I decided that we needed a FaQ section, and ran A/B tests on whether it should be on the home page or on the connect page since those seemed to be where the original users had the most questions.

A: Faq on landing
B: Faq on connect

Style Guide

I completely redid TSE’s original style, although there wasn’t much of a consistent style to begin with. I decided to incorporate a more minimal and fluid design while also adding a lot more personalized headers and photos. I wanted the website to act more than just a website to fill out applications in, but an embodiment of TSE, who they are, and what they are about.

Reflection

Although we did do some testing, I would like to have tested on actual users rather than random students. Ideally I would be able to test the website from this year’s fall recruitment where there are actual students who are interested in TSE. I would also like to test the website further with non-profit stakeholders which would allow me to fine tune the website even further.

Overall I’m very happy with how the redesign went. The rest of TSE also feels the same which is a great relief for myself. Currently I’m working on a redesign for their logo as well which will be coming soon!
