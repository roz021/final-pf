Exalton

Innovation Through Collaboration

March 2019 - May 2019

Role - Lead Designer

Teammates - Erik Hanzlick, Vidit Goyal

Desktop App Website Brand

Overview

Exalton is a file management platform that is leveraging emerging technologies to bring in a new wave of collaboration. Since each organization tends to work isolated in their own world, we found that innovation begins bottlenecking at trust. Now, imagine a world where anyone, anywhere, can collaborate with each other without the risk of piracy or theft. This is the world Exalton means to create.

Upon joining Exalton I realized that there was a lot more design work to do besides UI/UX, but I was excited to get my hands on a wider area of design. Since we were starting at a clean slate, I worked concurrently with the dApp to also build Exalton’s personality throughout the spring.

Designing
The Brand

Color

We spent a long time deliberating the colors we wanted to embody Exalton. I chatted with everyone on the team and ran an exercise where we imagined Exalton to be a person. What type of person would he/she be?

With the words that popped up most, we tried to see if those matching colors would feel appropriate.

Once we had the six colors, we knew we had to narrow it down. We wanted to see what would fit Exalton the best, so we were open to either a single tone or dual tone design. After discussing it further, we felt that have blue hues to symbolize Exalton’s core feature of being the most secure and most transparent file sharing platform would go well with an orange hue to symbolize the innovation that can happen through collaboration.

Logo

The next task was to use our colors and design a logo out of it. This was a really fun experience for me because I was able to be as creative as I wanted with it. Over the course of a week I started sketching and prototyping various logotypes to see which ones the team liked the best.

Once we narrowed down the choices to four, I continued to iterate over all of them. Eventually we ruled out the latter two. We felt that the isometric cube within a cube may have the most potential in terms of symbolizing what we felt Exalton is. Eventually we landed upon the final design.

The outer blue square represents the environment that Exalton provides. A transparent and secure workspace where innovators can become collaborators without fear of theft or piracy. The inner orange cube is the innovation that can occur from collaborating. At it’s core, Exalton is focused on what the users can do, not ourselves.

The play on perspective and the clear gaps are meant to accentuate the transparency of our application. There are no secrets here, only a place for great ideas to take form.

Reflection

Although I was brought onboard as just a UI/UX Designer, I was more than happy to test my hands at a broader area of design. I think this was a really great opportunity to test my creativity beyond drawing squares and circles, and it definitely proved to be challenging. I believe that from this I’ve learned a lot about branding and just how much it takes for any organization to try and create their brand.

In the future I would love to do a redesign for Exalton depending on how far we’ve grown and what still applies/no longer applies. One of the biggest things I learned from this was managing my time and prioritizing certain tasks. Several of these branding assets came at a moment’s notice, and I initially struggled to balance school and work. I am still very grateful to be a part of this team and I know that I will only continue to learn more.
