VENMO

Redesigning Clunky UX/UI

January 2019

Role - UX/UI Designer/Researcher

Overview

Venmo is an app that allows users to pay and request money from any other user. At its core, Venmo provides a way to pay off debts when cash is too cumbersome. I am an avid user myself, and have realized that Venmo seems to be plagued by issues. I gave myself two weeks to try and understand and diagnose these issues.

DESIGN PROCESS

Empathize

Interviews

I began the process by conducting a few in-depth interviews with my friends and family who I knew were prominent users of the app.

I wanted to be able to be thorough in order to catch most of the faults that exist in the app. Besides understanding who these users were, I also wanted to have them tell me any problems they have experienced already with the app (which were plentiful) and then conducting a usability test.

Almost all of my interviewees expressed some issue, or seemed to struggle with certain UI in the app.

Define

Personas

Pain Points

Problem Statement

Venmo’s app is plagued with UI issues. Much of the design seems obsolete or makes it harder for users to do what they intend. Lack of certain functionalities and presence of too many extraneous ones are leading to an unenjoyable UX.

Ideate

Prioritization

I used the MoSCoW Method in order to start prioritizing my ideas in terms of how feasible they were and how impactful they would be. My focus will be on the ones in dark blue.

Task Flow

I created two task-flows. One is based off of a card-sorting activity I conducted with users during research, and the other is a general flow for the whole app.

Paper Sketches

I proceeded to start sketching out the basic UI according to my flows, pesronas, and needs.

Prototype &
Testing

Wireframes

I started sketching out potential solutions to the pain points and how I can most effectively accomplish the goals that I had set. Being a prominent venmo user definitely made this part very easy.

Sketches converted into wires after some early evaluations with friends who use Venmo.

User Testing

After creating a few hi-fi prototypes I began evaluating them via Nielsen's Usability Heuristics. I also handed over the designs to three other colleagues to help me with this section. Once I was satisfied, I ran A/B tests on specific features I was unsure about.

Final Prototype

Prototype Demo

Validation

I returned to the initial group of interviewees and had them conduct a few tasks that highlighted the pain points I targeted. I followed up with a qualitative debrief to collect more data.

Based off of this feedback I believe I was successful in alleviating many of the critical issues Venmo currently has. However, there is always room for improvement. In future steps I may try to focus more on how to enhance the search functionality further through more user testing and ideation.
